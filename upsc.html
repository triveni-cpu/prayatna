Group examinations


Group examinations in India are competitive recruitment exams conducted by govt bodies to select candidates for various govt posts.
The term "GROUP" refers to the classification of posts based on their level of responsibility, authority, pay scale and role within the govt.
These are broadly divided into 2 levels. They are:


1.State-Level group Examinations
2. Central-Level group Examinations

State-Level Group Examinations
The state-level group examinations are conducted by the State Public Service Commission (SPSC) to recruit candidates for state govt jobs. These are generally classified as:
    Group 1 -
    Highest-level executive / administrative posts.
    Example: Deputy Collector, DSP, CTO, RDO, etc..,

    Group 2 -
    Executive / subordinate posts below Group 1.
    Example: Municipal Commissioner, Sub-Registor, Asst.Commercial Tax Officer(ACTO)etc..,

    Group 3 -
    clerical/ non-executive posts.
    Example: panchayat Secretary, junior Employment Officer, Auditor etc..,

    Group 4 -
    Lower division clerks / Assistants
    Example: junior Assistant, Typist, Record Assistant, etc..,
